eGallery

Many congratulations on finding this hidden page, an electronic exhibition showing my photography collection, I hope you can sense a new world through these images and digital pixels. The art of photography is still alive as an object of criticism, but what is more interesting is that it became a social and everyday life act. After the invention of the electronic camera, faster, cheaper and more portable devices were made available to the public, which launched the ambitions and curiosities of everybody. The concept of "photography as neurosis" is more helpful to understand photography in the context of the trend that everyone would like to press the shutter. The act of pressing the shutter is similar to obsessive-compulsive disorder and anxiety. When dealing with a scene, person, or food, the act of pressing the shutter is almost like a semi-automatic response, rather than a thoughtful act that produces a work.

Off Topic

To collect photographs is to collect the world. To photograph is to appropriate the thing photographed. It means putting oneself into a certain relation to the world that feels like knowledge and power. A now notorious first fall into alienation, habituating people to abstract the world into printed words, is supposed to have engendered that surplus of Faustian energy and psychic damage needed to build modern, inorganic societies. This generation of humans seems to be becoming less and less trusting of the brain, and less trusting of the eyes, and instead trusting in the lens and this box of technology, and they believe it is necessary to open the lens cap to archive and digitize the world.

I have to admit that photography has liberated the hands of artists. But mankind's exploration of the ideal world and the real world has never stopped. What is photography? Is it a record of reality or a depiction of the ideal? Where is the boundary between ideal and reality?
